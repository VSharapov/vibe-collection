Model notes
Each lab L evolves as: L[d] = L[d-1] − recL(L[d-1] − baselineL) + kL,terb·terb(d)·(1 + cum·daysOnTerb) + kL,alc·drinks(d) + kL,ibu·ibu(d) + kL,syn·(terb·drinks). Bilirubin couples to chronic ALT excess (Hy's-law-ish). Albumin sags only under sustained injury (28-day trailing). Sensitivities (k): ALT=ref, AST relatively more alcohol-sensitive, ALP slow + cumulative (cholestatic), bilirubin gated by ALT excess. Units arbitrary but scaled to look like clinical U/L & mg/dL.

Glossary

Terbinafine
Oral antifungal (brand: Lamisil); used for nail and skin fungal infections; rare but serious liver risk.
Hepatotoxicity
Drug-induced liver injury (DILI). Damage to liver cells caused by a medication or toxin.
Idiosyncratic
Unpredictable, host-specific reaction not strictly tied to dose. Most terbinafine liver injury is idiosyncratic.
Susceptibility (model)
Per-individual multiplier on terbinafine insult. Captures idiosyncratic risk; typical patient ≈1, rare responder 10–20.
Latency (DILI)
Time from drug start to injury onset. For terbinafine clinically significant injury, mean ≈32–34 days.
Hepatocellular pattern
Injury dominated by ALT/AST rise (hepatocyte damage), as opposed to cholestatic.
Cholestatic pattern
Injury dominated by ALP and bilirubin rise; reflects impaired bile flow. Terbinafine often mixed/cholestatic.
ALT (SGPT)
Alanine aminotransferase. Enzyme released when hepatocytes are damaged. Most specific common liver marker.
AST (SGOT)
Aspartate aminotransferase. Hepatocyte enzyme but also in muscle/heart; rises with liver or muscle injury.
ALP
Alkaline phosphatase. Rises with bile-duct injury or bone turnover. Marker of cholestatic liver injury.
GGT
Gamma-glutamyl transferase. Most sensitive serum marker of alcohol use; not on your panel and not simulated here.
Bilirubin (total)
Yellow pigment from heme breakdown; cleared by liver. High levels cause jaundice.
Bilirubin (direct/conjugated)
Fraction processed by the liver. Elevation suggests excretion problem (cholestasis, hepatocyte injury).
Albumin
Main blood protein made by the liver. Drops with chronic liver dysfunction (long half-life ~20 days).
Total protein
Sum of albumin and globulins; gives a coarse view of synthetic and immune protein status.
ULN / LLN
Upper / Lower Limit of Normal for a lab's reference range.
U/L, mg/dL, g/dL
Units per liter; milligrams or grams per deciliter (100 mL). Standard clinical lab units.
DILI
Drug-Induced Liver Injury. Umbrella term for any liver damage caused by drugs.
Hy's Law
Rule: ALT >3×ULN plus bilirubin >2×ULN without obstruction predicts ~10% mortality risk.
R-value
(ALT/ULN) ÷ (ALP/ULN). >5 hepatocellular, <2 cholestatic, in between mixed.
NSAID
Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug (ibuprofen, naproxen, etc.). Mild hepatic and renal effects with chronic use.
Synergy (drug interaction)
Combined effect greater than additive. Modeled here for terbinafine + alcohol on hepatocyte stress.
Cumulative factor
Model term: per-day terbinafine insult grows slowly with days-on-drug (proxy for tolerance loss / metabolite buildup).

Thresholds — what crossing them means

ALT or AST above ULN (50 U/L)
Mild "transaminitis." Transient rise occurs in <1% of terbinafine patients (Brown 2024; LiverTox). Usually monitored, not stopped.
Clinically significant terbinafine injury
Occurs in ~0.3–0.4% of courses; latency mean ≈32–34 days. Set susceptibility ≈10–20 to model.
ALT > 3× ULN (150 U/L)
Standard threshold for "clinically significant" elevation. Repeat labs; consider stopping the drug; look for symptoms.
ALT > 5× ULN (250 U/L)
Moderate hepatocellular injury. Most guidelines: stop the offending drug. Recheck labs in a few days.
ALT > 10× ULN (500 U/L)
Severe injury. Stop drug, evaluate urgently, rule out other causes (viral, autoimmune, ischemic).
ALT > 20× ULN (1000+ U/L)
Massive hepatocyte necrosis. ER-level. Concern for fulminant liver failure depending on synthetic function.
ALP > 2× ULN (~258 U/L)
Significant cholestasis. Common in mixed/cholestatic terbinafine injury, can outlast ALT rises by weeks.
Total bilirubin > ULN (1.2 mg/dL)
Beginning of hyperbilirubinemia. Subclinical jaundice typically appears around 2–3 mg/dL (scleral icterus).
Total bilirubin > 2× ULN with ALT > 3× ULN
Hy's Law positive. Strong predictor of serious DILI; mandatory drug stop and workup.
Albumin < 3.5 g/dL
Mild hypoalbuminemia. Many causes (inflammation, nutrition, kidney). With liver injury, suggests reduced synthetic function.
Albumin < 3.0 g/dL
Moderate. In sustained liver injury suggests subacute/chronic dysfunction. Slow lab — weeks-scale lag.
Albumin < 2.5 g/dL
Severe. Concerning for advanced chronic liver disease or major systemic illness.
AST > ALT ratio
"AST>ALT" pattern (ratio > ~1.5–2) classically suggests alcohol-related injury or advanced fibrosis/cirrhosis.
